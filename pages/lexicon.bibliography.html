Spectrophotometer

Spectrophotometers are colorimetric instruments used to capture and evaluate color. As part of a color control program control program, they are used to specify and communicate communicate color, while manufacturers use them to monitor color accuracy accuracy throughout production. Spectrophotometers are capable of measuring virtually any liquid sample, plastic, paper, metal or textile, and help to guarantee colour from design to delivery. There are three different spectrophotometers.

Trichromy

Trichromy is a process for reproducing a very large number of colors colors from three primary colors. Invented simultaneously by Charles Cros and Louis Ducos du Hauron, developed by the latter, it enables photography, cinema and television to be produced in color.

Direct dye

A direct dye or "substantive dye" is a soluble colorant used in dyeing. It allows direct dyeing of fibers of cellulosic origin. cellulose fibers. It is a generally inexpensive dye. Some direct dyes can also be used to dye wool and polyamides with good good fastnesses

Continuous/discontinuous dyeing

Diffusion dyeing processes (autoclave, jet, jigger, etc.) are referred to as "discontinuous dyeing". They require the fabric to be weighed fabric before placing it in a machine to be dip-dyed for a certain time. The dye bath contains the amount of water, dye and chemicals and chemicals required for dyeing. Once the shade has been achieved, the bath is drained and the fabric washed. Continuous dyeing processes enable higher yields to be achieved. efficiency. The technique works by dyeing through impregnation and spinning between two rollers

Vat dyes

Vat dyes are dyes used in dyeing on cellulose fibers and animal fibers. and animal fibers; in rare cases, they can also be applied to synthetic yarns. They are very strong dyes (Grand teint).

Metamerism

Metamerism is the ability of a hue to change its appearance according to its exposure to light. A colored surface inevitably receives light, we therefore use mainly non-metameric colors.

Migrations

Movement of color after completion of the dyeing process, from bag to garment, for example.
